Local-first inbox recall for AI agents

mail-index

An MCP server that gives your agent durable memory of your mailbox — without the keys to it.
The problem

Gmail search is weak.
Agents have
no memory.

Stock Gmail-API MCPs are lookup tools: exact query, a network round-trip per call, raw payloads dumped into the model's context. Ask a vague question and you get nothing back.

What mail-index is

Recall,
not lookup.

A local index over your whole inbox — fuzzy, ranked, snippet-first — so your agent answers the vague questions a query box can't.

Live · one local MCP call
How it works

Five moves

01 Sync — a preview of the whole mailbox in minutes; bodies fetched selectively
02 Graph — contacts, domains, threads; centrality over your human mail
03 Interest — an engagement score per contact, a seed for curation
04 Curate — you confirm who and what matters; that drives what's fetched
05 Query — your agent searches and reads, all locally, via MCP
Prewarm → serve local → fetch on miss
Measured on a real 6-month mailbox

15.3×
fewer tokens

Across a 100-question suite, mail-index answered every question for 15× fewer tokens than a stock Gmail-API MCP — and the gap widens exactly where lookup has no primitive at all.

Trust posture

Your mail never
leaves your machine.

▸ Local-first — the index lives on your disk, nowhere else
▸ Read-only — it never sends or mutates your mail
▸ No telemetry — the local-only promise is enforced in CI by an egress-guard test
